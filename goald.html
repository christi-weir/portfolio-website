goald is a money-saving app that uses psychology to help users reach their financial goals. Small, managable milestones paired with positive reinforcement makes saving easy and fun for the user.

Timeline

January 2021 - February 2021

UI/UX Designer

Project Strategy, User Research, Visual Design, Prototyping, Testing

Purpose

Saving money can be difficult, but with the right tools and proper encouragement it can be rather easy. This app is for users who are not new to technology and who are planning to save before a big expense. The user needs to be able to great a goal with a timeline and see all of their financial information to see if they are on track.

Context

Psychologists have been using a method of goal setting for decades that has proven to be elective. This method, known as SMART, is an acronym for the types of goals that are most e!ective: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Timely. By setting smaller objectives under the mentioned goal types, users are more likely to meet their saving goal. The app will use clean design, positive psychology, encouraging writing to keep users engaged with meeting their goals.

User Flow

User Stories

User Story #1: As a new user, I need to be able to create a profile, so that the financial advice I receive is personalized to me, my spending habits, and tailored to my goals.

User Story #2: As a user, I want to see a dashboard of my finances clearly and visually, so that I can see how much I am spending on what at a glance.

User Story #3: As a user, I need to be able to tell the tool what my savings goal is and how long I have to reach it, so that I can save accordingly.

Introduction & Scenario

Good day, Thank you for taking time to review my project. Please note that this test is in no way a test of your ability, it is to measure the usability of the product. Try to spend no more than 5-10 minutes going through the screens. This is a money saving app that focuses on setting and accomplishing goals in an easy and fun way. The screens you’ll be going through are the set-up screens new users will encounter. Imagine you are a new user who just downloaded the app. You want to create a profile, so that the financial advice you receive is personalized to you, your spending habits, and goals. Your task is to sign up with email, go through the screens that collect information to make the app experience custom to your needs, and end on your customized user dashboard. Any and all constructive criticism is appreciated as it will help me make my design the best it can be. Thank you!! Christi

Style Guide

Logo

The logo is and bold, featuring a gold coin against the blue background.

Colors

The colors I have chosen are blue which evokes feelings of loyalty and trust, and orange which evokes confidence and success. The color choices will bring out the main themes of the app, success and security, which highlight the user’s goal to grow their savings. Secondary colors will be used mainly for illustrations and only in less saturated values.

Typography

I have chosen a rounded, sans-serif font to be the major font for titles and headings.

Domus Regular - The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog

For body text, I chose a less round but still sans-serif font that looks clean and modern.

Hero New - The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog

Writing Style

The writing will be positive, encouraging, and will evoke a feeling of security. Since the app will focus on using psychology and the SMART method of goal setting, the writing will also include tips for staying on track. Loading screens can feature quotes or tips for effective goal success.

Image Style

The app will not feature any photographs but will have illustrations that are rounded and in the color theme. Images will be happy and encouraging. Icons will be minimal yet rounded in design.
